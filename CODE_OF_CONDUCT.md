# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

JourneyGraph aims to provide a respectful, technically rigorous, and welcoming environment for
people of all backgrounds and levels of experience. Participation in the project is a privilege
conditioned on treating others with dignity.

## Expected behavior

Participants should:

- communicate with patience, empathy, and professional respect;
- critique ideas, code, evidence, and trade-offs rather than people;
- assume good intent while still addressing concrete impact;
- welcome questions and explain unfamiliar project conventions;
- acknowledge mistakes, correct them, and preserve an accurate record;
- respect privacy, attribution, licenses, and confidential disclosures;
- keep discussions relevant to the project and its community;
- accept moderation decisions without retaliation.

Technical disagreement is normal. Strong claims should be supported with reproducible evidence,
and uncertainty should be stated plainly.

## Unacceptable behavior

The following are not acceptable in project spaces:

- harassment, threats, intimidation, stalking, or sustained disruption;
- discriminatory, sexualized, demeaning, or deliberately inflammatory language or imagery;
- personal attacks, insults, or unwelcome attention;
- publishing another person's private information without explicit permission;
- exposing security reports, credentials, traces, personal data, or confidential material;
- impersonation, deceptive attribution, plagiarism, or knowingly false claims;
- retaliation against someone who raises a concern in good faith;
- abuse of issue, review, moderation, or platform-reporting systems.

## Scope

This policy applies in repository issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions, commits,
documentation, project events, and other spaces where someone is representing JourneyGraph.
It also applies to private project communication when that communication affects participation
or safety in the community.

## Reporting a concern

For non-sensitive concerns, open a concise repository issue describing the behavior and the
project context. Do not repeat abusive content beyond what is necessary to identify it.

The repository does not currently publish a private conduct mailbox. If a report contains
personal, safety-sensitive, or confidential information, do not post it publicly. Use GitHub's
platform abuse or safety reporting tools and preserve relevant links privately. If the
repository later lists an official private moderation channel, prefer that channel.

Security vulnerabilities follow the separate [Security Policy](SECURITY.md).

## Enforcement

Authorized project moderators may take actions proportionate to the behavior and its impact,
including:

1. a private or public correction;
2. a warning and request to stop;
3. removal or editing of comments or contributions;
4. temporary participation restrictions;
5. permanent removal from project spaces;
6. escalation to the hosting platform where safety or platform policy is involved.

Moderators should document decisions sufficiently for consistent enforcement while protecting
reporters and affected people. Conflicts of interest should be disclosed, and another authorized
moderator should handle the matter when practical.

Reports made in bad faith, including fabricated evidence or retaliatory complaints, may
themselves violate this policy. A report is not bad faith merely because evidence is incomplete
or the concern is not ultimately substantiated.

## No retaliation

Retaliation against a person who reports a concern, assists a review, or declines unwanted
contact is prohibited. Good-faith reporters should not be pressured to disclose more personal
information than is needed for a fair review.

## Changes to this policy

Material changes to this policy should be reviewed publicly and recorded in the repository.
No contributor, sponsor, employer, or user is exempt from it.
